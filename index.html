Research

My research focuses on HI studies in nearby galaxies, FRB host galaxy identification, and low-frequency radio transients. I primarily work with radio data from the VLA, FAST, and LOFAR, and have extensive experience in both calibration and analysis of interferometric observations. Through these studies, I aim to improve our understanding of the gas content, variability, and transient phenomena revealed at radio wavelengths.

Current Topics

  • AVID project: investigating HI gas in nearby interacting and merging galaxies to study gas dynamics and galaxy evolution.
  • FRB host galaxies: identifying and characterising host galaxies of fast radio bursts through cross-matching and statistical association methods.
  • Radio transients: searching for and analysing low-frequency radio transients using LOFAR and related survey data.
